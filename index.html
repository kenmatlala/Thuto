Medical waste includes any waste generated during the diagnosis, treatment, or immunisation of humans or animals. This covers sharps (e.g. needles, blades), infectious waste, anatomical waste, expired medicines, chemicals, and cytotoxic substances.

Incorrect disposal can spread diseases such as HIV and hepatitis, contaminate the environment, and result in legal action. Safe disposal protects public health and ensures compliance.

Key legislation includes:

National Environmental Management: Waste Act (Act 59 of 2008)
Health Act, 2003
Occupational Health and Safety Act (Act 85 of 1993)
Environmental Conservation Act (Act 73 of 1989)

These laws outline how healthcare risk waste must be handled from generation to final disposal.

The waste generator — whether a clinic, hospital, dental practice, or vet — remains legally responsible for the waste until it is fully treated and safely disposed of, even when using third-party service providers.

Non-compliance can lead to:

  • Heavy fines

  • Practice closure

  • Environmental liability

  • Criminal charges
